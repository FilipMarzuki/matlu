# Matlu — World Bible

This document is the creative foundation for all lore writing in Matlu.
AI agents generating lore entries should read this fully before creating anything.

---

## Core premise

The player is a cleansing force moving through a world being consumed by corruption.
Corruption does not just destroy — it transforms, accelerates, and reveals what was already
wrong. On Earth, corruption speeds up a collapse that was already coming. On the alien worlds,
it mutates ecosystems that were already violent and strange.

The tone begins **cozy and familiar** and darkens with each world and each level.
The player should feel they have something to lose before they feel they are losing it.

---

## Core themes

- **Corruption as accelerant** — It does not create evil from nothing. It finds what is already
  fragile and broken and pushes it faster toward ruin. A struggling ecosystem collapses. A tense
  society fractures. A wounded creature becomes a predator.
- **The cost of cleansing** — Cleansing is not painless or clean. Something is lost each time,
  even when the right choice is made. Neutral kills carry weight.
- **Nature versus control** — Across all three worlds, the tension is between wild, living systems
  and the forces (corporate, mechanical, corrupted) that try to dominate or harvest them.
- **Small things matter** — A rabbit warren. A pond. A settlement at a forest edge.
  The game is won or lost in the accumulation of small acts, not one heroic moment.

---

## Writing style

- **Grounded first, then strange.** Start every description from something the reader recognises —
  an animal behaviour, a landscape feature, a human habit — then let the corruption or alienness
  emerge from that familiar base.
- **Spare prose.** Short sentences. No purple language. Let strangeness carry itself.
- **Specificity over generality.** "A fox with glass-white eyes that does not blink" beats
  "a corrupted fox with unusual eyes."
- **No omniscient narrator.** Write as an observer who has been in this world — someone with
  incomplete knowledge, not a god describing a game mechanic.
- **Avoid:** chosen one framing, cartoonish evil, exposition dumps, explaining what corruption
  is directly. Show what it does.

## The corruption

The corruption has no face and no agenda. It is a pressure — like heat, like gravity — that
finds weakness and amplifies it. Its origin is unknown within the game world. Characters have
theories; none are confirmed.

Corrupted entities are not evil versions of themselves. They are **extreme versions** — more
territorial, more hungry, more afraid, more aggressive. A corrupted fox is not a demon. It is
a fox whose fear response never turns off.

Visually: corruption manifests as **distortion at the edges**. Fur that grows the wrong direction.
Eyes that catch light they shouldn't. Movement that is slightly too fast, or that pauses in
wrong places. Colours that are almost right but not quite — a fox that is too orange, a crow
that is too black, water that is too still.

## Earth

### What it is

Earth is the player's home world. Level 1 takes place here. The landscape is a northern European
coastline — cold light, forest edges meeting the sea, small human settlements at river mouths and
valley floors. Familiar animals: foxes, crows, rabbits, deer, seabirds.

The player arrives when corruption has only just begun. Things are still recognisable. This is
deliberate — the player needs to feel at home before they feel the wrongness.

### The arc: contemporary → Mad Max + Cyberpunk

Earth does not hold. The corruption accelerates societal fracture that was already underway.
What might have taken a century of slow collapse happens in years or months. Lore entries
should reflect **where on this arc** they sit.

**Early / contemporary (Level 1):**
- Recognisable modern-day northern Europe
- Small towns, fishing communities, mixed forests
- Wildlife is abundant and mostly uncorrupted
- People are anxious but functional
- Factions: municipal authorities, conservation groups, local clans beginning to form at the edges
- Tone: cozy unease. Something is wrong but no one can name it.

**Mid collapse (implied, off-screen during Level 1):**
- Supply chains failing. Fuel scarce. Grid unreliable.
- Settlements begin to wall themselves. Some go dark.
- Corporate emergency contractors arrive with their own agenda.
- Corruption zones spreading. Wildlife fleeing or transforming.
- Tone: desperate improvisation. Mad Max aesthetics beginning to show — salvaged vehicles,
  armed checkpoints, barter replacing currency.

**Late / dystopia (future levels on Earth, or background lore):**
- Wasteland zones alternate with fortified corporate enclaves
- The enclaves have power, neon, surveillance, synthetic food. The wasteland has none of it.
- Clan societies have formed with their own rituals around the corruption — some worship it,
  some fight it, some harvest it.
- Corporate entities are extracting something from corrupted zones. Nobody outside knows what.
- Tone: high-tech cruelty alongside desperate survival. Cyberpunk aesthetics fully present —
  not chrome and rain, but solar panels on ruined rooftops and data pads on salvaged trucks.

### Factions (Earth)

**The Settlement Wardens** — loose alliance of town leaders trying to maintain pre-collapse
norms. Bureaucratic, well-meaning, increasingly irrelevant. Their language is still municipal.

**Scavenger clans** — formed from people who left or were expelled from settlements.
Highly mobile, tribal, pragmatic. Not villains — they are survivors. Suspicious of outsiders
and of anything that smells like authority.

**Meridian Logistics** — a corporate entity operating under emergency contracts.
They move supplies and they take payment in land rights and data. Polite, professional,
and not to be trusted. Their presence correlates with things getting worse.

**The Watchers** — a loose network of people who understood corruption before anyone else did.
No central leadership. Share information through coded channels. Some are scientists. Some are
not. They know things they are not saying.

### Writing Earth lore

When writing Earth entries, ground them in the **contemporary layer first** and let the
dystopian direction show as subtext or future implication — unless the entry is explicitly
set in the mid-collapse or late periods.

A fox living near a settlement in Level 1 does not know about Meridian Logistics.
It knows about bins and compost heaps and the smell of the sea.
Start there.

## Cross-world notes for lore agents

- Each world has a **pre-corruption baseline** and a **corrupted state**. When writing a new
  entry, be clear (implicitly) which state you are describing.
- Corruption looks different on each world. On Earth: distortion and hyper-aggression.
  On Spinolandet: simplification and loss of symbiosis. On Vattenpandalandet: TBD.
- Characters from one world encountering another is rare and significant — do not write
  cross-world meetings casually.
- The player's presence in a zone does not automatically mean the zone is safe. Lore entries
  can describe ongoing danger even in cleansed areas.
